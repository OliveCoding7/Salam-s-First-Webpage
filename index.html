My Favourite Palestinian Dishes

About Palestinian Food!

Palestinian food reflects a deep connection to land, family, and tradition, shaped by centuries of agricultural life and regional exchange. It highlights the use of grains, legumes, vegetables, and fragrant spices, creating a balance of hearty textures and bright, herbal notes. Meals often begin with shared small plates that encourage conversation and community. Slow-cooked specialties and oven-baked staples reveal techniques passed down through generations, while desserts blend subtle sweetness with warm aromatics. Throughout the cuisine, olive oil plays a central role, symbolizing both heritage and resilience. Overall, Palestinian cooking emphasizes generosity, seasonality, and the joy of gathering around food.

My Top 3

  • Maqlouba

    • Maqlouba is a flavorful, layered Middle Eastern dish of rice, vegetables, and meat, flipped before serving to create a beautiful, structured presentation.

  • Hummus

    • Hummus is a creamy Middle Eastern spread made from blended chickpeas, tahini, lemon, and garlic, enjoyed with bread or vegetables.

  • Malfouf

    • Malfouf is a traditional dish of cabbage leaves stuffed with spiced rice and sometimes meat, then rolled tightly and slowly cooked in tangy broth.
